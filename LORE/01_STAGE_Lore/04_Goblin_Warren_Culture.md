## The Mud Pits: Mire-Wells
Deep within the soot-caked underbelly of the Aurelian Heartland ruins, where the air is heavy with the smell of boiling tallow and rotten copper, lie the Mire-Wells. The entire Goblin species crawls fully formed out of these wild, bubbling alchemical mud pits. Goblins allow the raw, black alchemical sludge of the earth-blood fissures to flood the dark caverns naturally, adapting their shanties directly to the shifting, toxic shoreline.

Rubbery, soot-stained pods swell in the warm, mineral-rich mud, ballooning under the constant heat of the deep steam vents. When a pod reaches its limit, it splits open with a wet, hissing pop. The newborn Goblin crawls out into the dark muck, gasping in the sulfurous air. The physical appearance of the hatchling is shaped entirely by the chemical quirks of the specific pit: Goblins born in the flooded, stagnant sumps of the old sewers emerge with slick, green, amphibian skin and webbed digits, while those spawned near the dry, volcanic vents of the ancient furnaces hatch with thick, soot-crusted hides that shrug off flying sparks.

## Flesh-Memory: The Wordless Knack
When a Goblin dies, the gang carries the remains back to the local Mire-Well and hurls them into the bubbling sludge. The acidic, alchemical properties of the raw Black Ichor dissolve the flesh within hours, but the flesh-memory of the deceased—the hardened skin, the calluses, the muscle memory, and the neural pathways forged by a lifetime of survival—remains permanently suspended in the mineral mire.

This heritage manifests in the newborn Goblin as a wordless, instinctual reflex. When a newly hatched Goblin first grips a heavy iron wrench or a scrap-musket, their fingers simply know the correct angle to turn the valve or pull the trigger. Grudges and fears are passed down as physical impulses; a Goblin walking through the ruins may catch sight of a human soldier wearing the crest of the old Aurelian Cartel, and their chest will tighten with a sudden, hot wave of wordless hatred, driven by the memory of the whip-cracks their ancestors endured.

This bloodline pool can be corrupted through acts of violence. During turf wars between rival Goblin **Gangs**, raiders will steal the corpse of an enemy boss or master-tinkerer and hurl it into their own mud pit. This act of corporate theft, known as Mire-Poisoning, allows the gang to absorb the rival's technical secrets. However, the mixture of conflicting lineages causes a condition known as Mire Schizophrenia. The resulting hatchlings suffer from competing physical reflexes: a Goblin's left hand may move to wire a steam furnace while the right hand tries to slit the Goblin's own throat, the body physically locked in a civil war between the muscle memories of two dead enemies.

## The Proving: The Schoolyard of the Warrens
A newly hatched Goblin, known as "Fresh Meat," enters an informal, brutal hierarchy dominated by a schoolyard bully dynamic. The hatchlings gather in the central, soot-darkened vaults surrounding the mud pits, where the older, bigger Goblins push them around, steal their salvaged copper bits, and force them to perform the dirtiest, most dangerous maintenance work. 

The Proving is a raw social struggle of informal hazing. Goblins who are too weak, slow, or submissive succumb to the bullying, spending their lives at the bottom of the social ladder, performing domestic tasks in the Lair—cleaning the grease traps, hauling coal, and carrying gear for their masters.

Those Goblins who are tough, quick, or clever enough to stand up for the Gobbos, hit back, or outsmart the bullies earn social status. They are the ones who get noticed by the gang lieutenants and recruited into the active **Mobs** for surface raids. To mark their departure from the low-status herd, a Goblin must commit a "Naming Act"—such as pulling off a successful theft or bringing back a heavy piece of alchemical **Loot** from the surface. Only then do they earn their first name and join the syndicate as a full member.

When a highly skilled Goblin feels the physical body slowing down due to injury or age, the veteran hosts a rowdy, alcohol-fueled feast with the **Gang** to prepare for the transition. At the height of the celebration, surrounded by the screaming cheers of the gang building up a wall of noise, the veteran leaps directly into the Mire-Well, melting the flesh while the alchemical skills are at peak efficiency to ensure the next generation inherits those reflexes.

## Ichor-Scribing: Tattoos of the Mire
To permanently alter their biology, Goblins practice a painful alchemical art known as **Ichor-Scribing**. 

Using sharp bone needles, alchemical specialists tap raw, unrefined Black Ichor directly into Goblins' skin. Because of their natural immunity to the sludge, the Ichor does not cause the flesh to rot. Instead, the alchemical grease bonds permanently with the blood and nervous system, forming thick, dark, slightly raised obsidian patterns across their bodies. 

The alchemical ink within these tattoos reacts to the presence of Sol-Quartz energy, physically hardening their hide against blades, granting them the ability to see in pitch darkness, or shielding their lungs from toxic gas. Crucially, because these tattoos physically warp the Goblin's bloodline traits, the modifications are preserved when the Goblin dies and is melted down in the Mire-Well. The alchemical soot of the tattoo dissolves into the mud, ensuring that future hatchlings crawl out of the slime with those physical gifts already built into their flesh.

## The Soot-Fringes: Surface Rails and Boar-Wagons
Goblins maintain a heavy, active presence on the surface ruins, claiming the "Soot-Fringes"—the vine-choked industrial suburbs, overgrown railway yards, and swampy estuaries surrounding the ancient cities. 

They construct chaotic, multi-level shantytowns inside the roofless shells of gothic foundries, hanging their leather hammocks from monumental brass pipes and iron arches. To move through the ruins, Goblins construct hand-cranked flatcars and armored rail-scooters to travel along the surviving imperial tracks, hauling heavy alchemical **Scrap** and machinery back to their outposts. 

For off-road travel, Goblins build mobile shanties and heavy wagons pulled by tusked forest boars. These tusk-boars are bad-tempered, thick-hided beasts that Goblins tame through brute force and alchemical bait. The boar-wagons are built of dark oak, decorated with polished brass plates and tattered velvet drapes stolen from the ruined estates of the High Aurelians. 

The Goblin caravans operate in a state of unstable neutrality. They act as mobile merchants, trading salvaged **Oddities** and bootleg fuel canisters with human Squatters and Colonists at the Grey Ports. Goblins are opportunistic. If they spot a weak human caravan or a merchant wearing a shiny brass monocle, they will pivot instantly, launch a screaming ambush to plunder the convoy, and splay back into the safety of the overgrown ruins.
