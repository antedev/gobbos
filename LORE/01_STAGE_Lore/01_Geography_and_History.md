# Geography and History

*The old world did not end with a scream or a fire. It ended when the hum stopped. The great glass towers went quiet, the immortal masters turned to dust in their chairs, and the pipes backed up with black poison. Now, the children of the colonies sail back in their wooden ships, looking for the golden age. They do not know that the golden age was built on the backs of their ancestors, and run by the green hands of the underbelly.*

## World Geography: The Two Continents
The known world is split by a wide, treacherous ocean into two distinct landmasses: the Broken Heartland and the Sovereign Arch-Provinces.

### The Broken Heartland
The Broken Heartland is the eastern continent and the former core of the Sol-Aurelian Empire. Geographically, the Heartland spans approximately 360,000 square miles (roughly equivalent in size to the United States East Coast). 
*   **The Barrier Mountains**: A sheer, impassable mountain range forms the western boundary of the Heartland. The mountains crawl with ravenous trolls, giants, and prehistoric beasts, hemming the civilized coastline in.
*   **The Coastal Plains**: The region between the Barrier Mountains and the ocean. This area contains the overgrown ruins of the Empire's white marble cities, collapsed railway tracks, and derelict factories.
*   **The Underbelly**: A subterranean network of maintenance tunnels, sewers, and refinery vaults running directly beneath the surface ruins. This is the domain of the Goblins.

### The Sovereign Arch-Provinces
The Sovereign Arch-Provinces is the western continent, situated across the ocean. Geographically, the continent spans roughly 1.5 million square miles (equivalent in size to Western and Central Europe).
*   **Origin**: Originally established as distant maritime colonies of the Sol-Aurelian Empire to extract timber, coal, wool, and metals.
*   **Current State**: Because the colonies lacked the primary Sun-Glass mirror spires and were not directly dependent on the central energy grid, they survived the collapse. Over the past century, they have stabilized into a collection of competing, wealthy, late-medieval and early-modern kingdoms and merchant republics. 
*   **Technology Level**: The Arch-Provinces operate at a mid-1600s European level, utilizing matchlock muskets, pike-and-shot formations, three-masted wooden galleons, and banking systems.

## History of the Sol-Aurelian Empire
The Sol-Aurelian Empire rose from a league of classical city-states on the Heartland coast into a global industrial superpower. Its history spans three centuries, divided into four distinct eras.

### Era I: The Cage of Dawn (Years 0–50)
The ancestors of the Empire were agrarian city-states hemmed in by the Barrier Mountains and the sea. Running out of wood and arable land, imperial miners dug deep into the mountain roots.
*   **The Discovery**: Imperial miners broke into a massive subterranean abyss. They discovered two resources: vast, primitive tribes of Goblins, and a massive lake of cold, black, metallic sludge known as Black Ichor.
*   **The Breakthrough**: Alchemists realized that Black Ichor, when combined with a rare, milk-white crystal called Sol-Quartz and targeted by concentrated sunlight, stabilized into a hyper-dense, heat-generating fuel. They built the first Sun-Glass mirror arrays to channel sunlight deep into the earth.

### Era II: The Great Subjugation (Years 50–150)
The Empire required a massive workforce to dig the tunnels and maintain the hot, toxic refineries. The citizens of the Empire were aristocratic and refused to work in the soot.
*   **The Goblin Draft**: The Empire launched campaigns into the deep tunnels, capturing Goblins by the thousands. Goblins were small enough to fit inside the narrow pipe networks and naturally resilient to alchemical fumes. Goblins were bred as a generational maintenance class.
*   **The Industrial Boom**: With limitless energy and slave labor, the Empire built soaring marble cities on the surface while miles of brass-piped factory complexes boomed beneath them. Flawless crucible steel was standardized.

### Era III: The Maritime Expansion (Years 150–250)
With the home continent fully controlled, the Empire turned its eyes across the ocean.
*   **The Conquered Colonies**: The Empire built iron-ribbed, steam-hybrid ships driven by alchemical fuel, establishing the colonies in the Sovereign Arch-Provinces.
*   **The Mercantile Trap**: The Empire did not conquer the colonies by force; they flooded them with cheap, mass-produced tools, textiles, and alchemical fuel canisters. The colonies became dependent on the Heartland for replacement parts and refined canisters while shipping raw lumber, coal, and gold back to the Empire.

### Era IV: The Tainted Rot (Years 250–300)
By Year 250, the natural reservoirs of Black Ichor began running dry. Refineries burned empty stone, and the global supply chain began to stutter.
*   **The Taint**: Desperate to keep the grid running, imperial scientists discovered they could artificially generate alchemical fuel by feeding human lives into the extraction vents. The legal system was weaponized, expanding capital crimes to ensure a steady supply of prisoners.
*   **The Creeping Rot**: The artificial fuel was unstable and carried a spiritual corruption. Refineries leaked toxic dross, and a creeping sickness spread through the working class.

## The Cataclysm: The Grand Desynchronization
Exactly 100 years ago, the alchemical infrastructure suffered a catastrophic mechanical failure.

*   **The Snapping**: To meet the energy quotas of the colonies, the state overloaded the central refineries. The alchemical connection between the crystals snapped.
*   **The Fracture**: Every Sol-Quartz crystal in existence fractured internally, filled with billions of microscopic cracks. They lost their ability to hold a charge.
*   **The Chime-Death**: Without the stabilizing harmonic frequency of the crystals, the ruling class (whose biology was mutated by the energy) aged centuries in a matter of months, withering into dust.
*   **The Fall**: The automated cities died instantly. Without maintenance, the steam lines ruptured, flooding the surface with toxic dross. The colonies, cut off from replacement parts, slid into a dark age of starvation and regression.

## The Heartland Today
A century after the collapse, the Heartland has transformed into a post-Roman, gothic-fantasy wilderness. Nature has reclaimed the stone. Towering white marble arches pierce ancient forests, and aqueducts span green valleys. Faint heat hazes, known as the **Solar Afterglow**, shimmer over the ruins.

### Demographic Balance
The current population of the Heartland consists of three main groups:

| Faction Group | Population | Role in the Heartland |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Underground Goblins** | ~4 Million | The dominant underworld power, living in the tunnels and salvaging the ruins. |
| **Surface Squatters** | ~2 Million | Descendants of the low-tier human servants, living in fortified ruins. |
| **Returning Colonists** | Variable | Armed expeditions from the Arch-Provinces trying to reclaim the technology. |
