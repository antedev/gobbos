# Geography and History

*The old world did not end with a scream or a fire. It ended when the hum stopped. The great glass towers went quiet, the immortal masters turned to dust in their chairs, and the pipes backed up with black poison. Now, the children of the colonies sail back in their wooden ships, looking for the golden age. They do not know that the golden age was built on the backs of their ancestors, and run by the green hands of the underbelly.*

## World Geography: The Two Continents
A wide, storm-swept ocean splits the known world into two distinct geographical landmasses: the ruined Aurelian Heartland and the independent Western Kingdoms.

### The Aurelian Heartland
The Aurelian Heartland is the eastern continent, representing the cradle and former core of the fallen **Aurelian Empire** (also remembered as the **Empire of the Sun**). Spanning approximately 360,000 square miles, this land is hemmed in to the west by the sheer, black basalt peaks of the Barrier Mountains. These mountains crawl with ravenous trolls, giants, and prehistoric beasts, keeping the civilized coastline isolated from the unexplored interior. 

Between the mountains and the sea lie the coastal plains, containing the overgrown, vine-choked ruins of the Empire's white marble cities, collapsed railway tracks, and silent factories. Directly beneath the cobblestones lies the underbelly—a subterranean labyrinth of maintenance tunnels, sewer lines, and alchemical vats that serves as the home of the Goblins.

### The Western Kingdoms
Across the ocean lies the western continent, home to the Western Kingdoms. Spanning roughly 1.5 million square miles, this continent was originally established as a network of distant colonies to supply the Heartland with timber, coal, and raw ore. Having survived the alchemical collapse of the core due to their lack of solar Mirror Spires, these territories have spent the last century evolving into a fragmented collection of independent feudal realms, free mercantile cities, and religious crown-states. They share a cultural memory of the golden age, but they are deep rivals, launching separate, competing expeditions back across the sea to plunder the ruins of their former masters.

## History of the Aurelian Empire
The Empire rose from a league of classical coastal city-states into a global industrial superpower. Its three-century history is divided into four distinct eras:

### Era I: The Cage of Dawn (Years 0–50)
The early city-states were agrarian, hemming themselves against the Barrier Mountains. Running out of wood and metal, imperial miners dug deep into the mountain roots, breaking into a massive subterranean abyss. Here, they discovered vast tribes of Goblins and a massive, cold lake of black, metallic sludge known as Black Ichor. The empire's alchemists discovered that when this inert sludge was pressurized inside milk-white Sol-Quartz crystals and targeted by concentrated sunlight, it output extreme, constant heat. They built the first Sun-Glass Mirror Spires to focus solar energy deep into the earth.

### Era II: The Great Subjugation (Years 50–150)
To run the toxic refinery vaults, the imperial military launched campaigns to capture the Goblin tribes. Small enough to crawl through the narrow pipe networks and biologically immune to alchemical fumes, Goblins were bred as a generational maintenance class. With limitless energy and slave labor, white marble cities rose on the surface while miles of brass-piped factory complexes boomed beneath them. Flawless crucible steel and early mechanical firearms became standardized.

### Era III: The Maritime Expansion (Years 150–250)
With the home continent locked down, the Empire built iron-ribbed, steam-hybrid ships to cross the ocean, establishing the colonies that would become the Western Kingdoms. The Empire subdued these foreign lands through trade monopolies, flooding them with cheap mass-produced steel tools, alchemical canisters, and textiles in exchange for raw timber, coal, and gold.

### Era IV: The Tainted Rot (Years 250–300)
As the natural pools of Black Ichor ran dry, the Empire weaponized its legal system, expanding capital crimes to feed human prisoners into the extraction vents to artificially generate fuel. This artificial fuel carried a spiritual corruption, causing the "Black Veins" sickness among the working class and leaking toxic dross into the soil.

## The Cataclysm: The Grand Desynchronization
Exactly 100 years ago, the alchemical network collapsed. Over-extraction caused a silent, planet-wide resonance wave to snap through the alchemical matrix. Every Sol-Quartz crystal in existence fractured internally, losing its ability to hold a stable charge. 

Without the grid's harmonic frequency, the golden-eyed High Aurelians (whose biology was mutated by the energy) aged centuries in a matter of months, withering to dust. The automated cities died instantly, flooding the surface with backing-up sewage and toxic dross. The colonies, cut off from alchemical canisters and replacement parts, collapsed into a century-long regression.

## The Heartland Today
A century after the collapse, the Heartland has transformed into a gothic-fantasy wilderness. Nature has reclaimed the stone, and the **Solar Afterglow** shimmers over the marble ruins. The region is a lawless theater of friction:
*   **The Goblins**: The dominant underworld power, numbering roughly 4 million, ruling the sewers and the "Soot-Fringes" of the surface ruins.
*   **The Squatters**: Roughly 2 million humans left behind, descendants of the Low Provincials, living in fortified ruins under the rule of local warlords known as **Scrap-Barons**.
*   **The Western Kingdoms**: Competing fleets of wooden galleons arriving from across the sea, armed with matchlock muskets, eager to strip-mine the ruins and recover the alchemical technology of the past.
