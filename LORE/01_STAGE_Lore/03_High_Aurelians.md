# The High Aurelians

*“They stood twice as tall as any of us, with eyes that burned like gold coins. They did not sweat, they did not tire, and they lived for three hundred years. We thought they were gods. But when the great hum died, they did not ascend to the sun—they shriveled like dry leaves in a hearth.”*
— *Scavenger-Scholar Thomas of House Vane*

## Biological Caste System
Generations of daily exposure to the refined Sol-Quartz energy grid rewrote the biology of the Aurelian Heartland's population. This created a rigid, biological caste system based on proximity to the mirror towers:

### 1. The High Aurelians
The ruling class of the Empire proper, residing in the sun-drenched capital cities.
*   **Physiology**: Towering figures standing seven feet tall. They possessed golden-irised eyes and hair resembling spun brass wire.
*   **Longevity**: Tripled lifespans. A High Aurelian remained in physical youth for up to 300 years and was entirely immune to standard diseases.
*   **Social Status**: They viewed themselves as living demi-gods, using their physical superiority to justify absolute rule.

### 2. The Low Provincials
The ordinary human citizens living in the rural fringes of the Aurelian Heartland and the Western Kingdoms across the sea.
*   **Physiology**: Standard human stature and lifespans. Because Sol-Quartz crystals were rare and reserved for heavy industry in their regions, they received no biological upgrade.
*   **Social Status**: Foremen, overseers, and garrison soldiers. They looked up at the High Aurelians with a mixture of awe and growing resentment.

### 3. The Underbelly (Goblins)
Enslaved laborers captured from subterranean caverns and forced into industrial servitude.
*   **Physiology**: Small, green-skinned, and short-lived. They worked in the unshielded vaults, shoveling corrosive dross and clearing pipes.
*   **Social Status**: Viewed as sub-human pests and assets rather than individuals.

## The Imperial Tether and the Chime-Sickness
The biological grace of the High Aurelians came with a severe, physical constraint known as the Chime-Sickness.

*   **The Tether**: Their mutated biology was dependent on the silent, high-frequency harmonic vibration emitted by the active Sol-Quartz grid.
*   **Chime-Sickness**: If a High Aurelian traveled away from the grid for more than a few weeks, their cells began to collapse. They suffered an aching, hollow fatigue, their golden eyes turned dull grey, and they underwent rapid, catastrophic aging, withering to ash.
*   **The Golden Cage**: Because the ruling class could not leave the Aurelian Heartland without dying, they were prisoners of their own utopia. They were forced to govern the Western Kingdoms through proxy governors and corporate mercantilism.

## The Law of Refinement
The state religion, **The Radiant Synod**, was established to justify the biological inequality of the Empire. The Synod codified a moral philosophy known as **Spiritual Density**:

*   **The Doctrine**: The universe is naturally born corrupt, heavy, and crude. The highest moral good is **Refinement**—the act of purifying the rough into the pristine.
*   **Spiritual Refinement**: The Synod preached that the 300-year lifespan and golden eyes of the High Aurelians were not a physical reaction to the crystals, but absolute proof of a refined, pure spirit.
*   **The Opaque**: Low Provincials were defined as "Opaque"—possessing heavy, dense souls that failed to refract the sun's light.
*   **The Disposable Filters**: The Goblins were framed as creatures of the deep mud, whose natural role was to labor in the soot to distill absolute purity for their masters.

## Surviving Lineages: The Gothic Factions
When the Grand Desynchronization fractured the crystals, the harmonic frequency died. Deprived of their tether, the majority of the High Aurelians withered into dust within months. Only a few hundred survived by turning to desperate, alchemical, or monstrous methods. They remain in the ruins as five distinct factions:

### 1. The Ichor-Gorged Tyrants (Solar Vampires)
Desperate nobles who abandoned the light and turned to the subterranean vaults.
*   **Survival Method**: They discovered that drinking alchemically altered doses of raw Black Ichor halts cellular aging, at the cost of complete physical corruption.
*   **Current State**: Bloated, monstrously tall, and pale, with black fluid pulsing through their veins. They rule the deep refinery ruins like feudal drug-lords, damming the Ichor fissures to feed their addiction.

### 2. The Crystal Cloistered (Fading Ascendants)
Aristocrats who refused to pollute their blood with Ichor.
*   **Survival Method**: They secluded themselves inside sealed vaults containing the last intact, unfractured Sol-Quartz arrays in the Aurelian Heartland.
*   **Current State**: Fragile, ghost-like shut-ins. They live inside their shrinking crystal fields, knowing that stepping outside into the clean air of the Aurelian Heartland will turn them to ash in minutes.

### 3. The Blind Zealots (Solar Fundamentalists)
Fanatical ascetics who fled to the highest peaks and scorched deserts.
*   **Survival Method**: They staved off the Chime-Sickness by exposing themselves directly to unshielded, raw sunlight at the top of broken spires.
*   **Current State**: Physically scorched and completely blind from staring directly into the sun. They are fanatically mad, wandering the ruins as walking batteries of raw solar energy.

### 4. The Brass Reliquaries (Iron-Bound)
Decaying commanders and high priests who turned to industrial medicine.
*   **Survival Method**: They arrested their cellular decay by permanently sealing their withered bodies inside alchemically pressurized clockwork suits.
*   **Current State**: Walking, steam-belching armor sets of brass and iron. They pilot these mobile shrines through the ruins, executing squatters to reclaim their old municipal districts.

### 5. The Exiles of the Western Kingdoms (Diluted Bloodlines)
Fugitives who successfully fled across the ocean in the final days before the collapse.
*   **Survival Method**: They intermarried with the wealthy merchant elite of the Western Kingdoms.
*   **Current State**: Modern humans who carry faded, diluted traits (such as pale golden eyes). They use their families' banking houses to fund the military expeditions returning to the Aurelian Heartland, eager to reclaim their ancestral birthright.
