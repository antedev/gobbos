A goblin Mob is defined by a few characteristics. 

First and foremost the size. The size determines the base capacity that the mob has. The other big part of it is Type. Type is what kind of gobbos there is in the mob. Gobbos are very skeptical on other gobbos, so a mob can only consist of one Type at any one point in time. 

## Sizes
Goblin mobs comes in different sized. Keeping a mob of goblins together is no easy task, and require a leader with enough Grunt. Otherwise they will simply not listen, and in worst case a ambitious Goblin might size the opportunity to turn the mob against the weak boss and try to take his place. 
#### Goblins strong together! 
The size of a goblin mob is not an exact science, so a goblin mob of size 1 is not just a simple goblin, but perhaps 3-8 goblins, whereas a size 5 is closer to 30-50 goblins.

When a mob reduces in size, from damage or a split, any excess loot they are carrying beyond the new capacity has to be dropped. The Boss might choose what item is dropped. 
## Traits
Goblins are driven by strong feelings and sudden urges. Sitting still and doing nothing is not their best trait. Instead their needs are driven by urges to eat stuff, steal stuff, break stuff, kill weak opponents and run away from strong opponents. A Goblin Mob that is without a strong leader and clear objective, will be more likely to follow these feelings. 

Which of the traits any one goblin will follow depends on the current situation they find themself in. This is affected by nearby enemies and allies, any condition they my be affected by and the zone layout of the battle map. 

As a baseline goblins prioritize the following: 
**Survival** > **Loot/Eat** > **Violence** > **Trash stuff** > **Wander off**
Talents of the boss or other effects might however change their priorities
#### Survival 
If there is a enemy in the Goblin zone that is mightier (TODO; how to calculate that?), they will first and foremost run away. They will use their actions to move to a safer zone, away from enemies, and preferably to A) other goblin mobs (not the Boss) or B) cover. This might trigger a Merge or move further away from the Boss. 
If they are cornered and cannot make any move, they will get Feral and fight. 
#### Loot/Eat
If there is no immediate danger, the goblins will try to either eat something or loot something, whatever it closest. If they have the capacity, they will pick up as much loot as they can carry. If they instead find food of some sort, they will set down what they have and eat, thus heal on 1d6 points if they have taken damage. 
#### Violence
If they have a beatable foe close-by, and there is nothing to eat or steal, they will attack that foe, charging it if it is in an adjacent zone. 
#### Thrash stuff
If non of the options above is available, the goblins will simply trash stuff. Especially if they are in a environment such as in a house or similar. But they are not picky and might as well trash some bushes or whatever is close by. 
#### Wander off
Finally, if they are truly in lack of anything to do, they will simply wander off, either by GMs choosing or a random direction. But usually this will mean going in a direction where there might be food, (an easy) fight or loot.
