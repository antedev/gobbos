# Core Design Definition: Tags in Gobbos

*If you set a zone on fire, it burns. If you rub poison on a blade, it sickens. If you shout a word of frost, it freezes. In Gobbos, these physical forces are called Tags. They are the systemic glue that connects magic, crafting, combat, and the environment.*

This document establishes the official design definition of a **Tag** in Gobbos, what it represents, and how it differs from other rules categories.

---

## 1. What is a Tag?

A **Tag** (always written in brackets, e.g., `[Fire]`, `[Sticky]`, `[Toxic]`) is the **systemic connective tissue** of the Gobbos game engine. It represents a **dynamic, physical force, element, substance, or magical state** that can be slapped onto or stripped away from creatures, gear, and zones.

Unlike static classifications, a Tag represents a *transient condition of matter or energy*. 

---

## 2. The Three Pillars of a Tag

Every Tag in Gobbos is defined by three fundamental design principles:

### Pillar 1: Dynamic Slapping (Slack-Attach)
Tags are not permanent. They are designed to be dynamically attached, removed, or transferred at the table:
*   You can attach `[Fire]` to a zone (the room is burning).
*   You can attach `[Fire]` to a sword chassis during downtime (crafting a fire crystal).
*   You can attach `[Fire]` to a creature for a round (the goblin is on fire).
*   You can throw a bucket of water (`[Wet]`) to strip the `[Fire]` tag away.

### Pillar 2: Symmetrical Rules Meaning
A Tag has the exact same baseline mechanical meaning whether it originates from a spell, an item, or a hazard:
*   If a wizard shouts the Power Word `[Sticky]`, it inflicts *Restrained*.
*   If a crafter attaches a spider gland (`[Sticky]`) to a net, it inflicts *Restrained*.
*   If a player steps into a mud pool (`[Sticky]`), they gain the *Restrained* condition.
There are no separate spell lists or item rules; there is only the tag glossary.

### Pillar 3: Narrative-First Chemistry (Synthesis)
Tags represent actual physical elements in the fiction. Because they have physical reality, they participate in **Element Synthesis** when they meet:
*   A `[Wet]` goblin hit by a `[Shock]` attack triggers conduction (attacks become Easy).
*   A `[Sticky]` net hit by `[Fire]` burns away, clearing the trap but dealing damage.
*   The GM and players resolve these interactions using common sense and the standard synthesis guidelines, not complex tables.

---

## 3. What Tags are NOT (The Contrast)

To prevent rules confusion, we separate Tags from two other core game concepts:

*   **You do not *synthesize* Ancestries or Traits.** 
*   **You do not *slap* Ancestries onto weapons.**

```
   [Fire]          = A TAG (slapped onto a sword, ignites a zone, can be put out).
   Bashing         = A TRAIT (built into a mace, alters combat math, cannot be put out).
   Undead          = An ANCESTRY (defines what a skeleton is, grants poison immunity).
```

---

## 4. The Core Tag Catalog (The Alphabet of Chaos)

For the core rulebook, we restrict the list of Tags to a small, highly interactive set of physical elements:

1.  **`[Fire]`**: Thermal energy. Baseline: Deals +1 success and ignites zones.
2.  **`[Sticky]`**: Adhesion. Baseline: Inflicts the *Restrained* condition.
3.  **`[Toxic]`**: Biological poison. Baseline: Inflicts the *Weakened* condition.
4.  **`[Chilled]`**: Sub-zero temperature. Baseline: Reduces movement by 1 Zone.
5.  **`[Shock]`**: Electrical discharge. Baseline: Arcs damage to an adjacent target.
6.  **`[Angelic]`**: Holy/healing energy. Baseline: Makes attacks against standard foes Easy (4+).
7.  **`[Spooky]`**: Spiritual dread. Baseline: Inflicts the *Terrified* condition.
8.  **`[Wet]`**: Liquid saturation. Baseline: Clears fire, conducts electricity.
9.  **`[Slick]`**: Frictionless grease. Baseline: Knocks targets *Prone* when moving.
