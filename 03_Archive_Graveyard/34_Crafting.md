# Crafting Custom Gear

Goblins rarely settle for standard equipment when there is perfectly good garbage lying around. By combining base weapons and armor with unique Oddities, a Gobbo can create devastating (and highly volatile) Custom Gear.

## The Components
Crafting requires two distinct components: a Base Item and an Oddity.

*   **Base Item:** Any standard Weapon or Armor piece (e.g., Heavy Weapon, Light Armor). This provides the item's core stats such as Bulk, Handedness, and base Damage or Passive Defence Dice. 
*   **Oddities:** Unique, bizarre pieces of salvage found in the world. Each Oddity possesses a mechanical Tag that alters how the Base Item functions.

## Assembly 
Goblins can attach Oddities to Base Items during downtime or out of combat.

### The Brains Limit
A piece of gear can only hold so much chaos before it becomes entirely unwieldy. 
* A character can attach a maximum number of Oddities based on their **Brains** stat. 
* Oddities attached to a Base Item do not consume Utility Slots or increase the item's Bulk unless explicitly stated by the Oddity's Tag.

> **Example:** Snark the Boss has Brains 2. He can tape a maximum of two unique Oddities to his standard Medium Weapon. If he tries to add a third, the weapon becomes impossibly complicated for him to wield, and functions as standard Junk until an Oddity is removed.

## The Quality Die
When an Oddity is attached to a Base Item, the weapon or armor is no longer subject to standard "Goblin Made" breaking rules involving Gear Dice. Instead, it becomes a dangerously unpredictable piece of Custom Gear.

Whenever a character uses Custom Gear for a test (e.g., attacking with a Custom Weapon or defending with Custom Armor), they must roll a number of **Quality Dice** (distinct, differently colored d6s) equal to the number of Oddities attached to the Base Item alongside their normal dice pool.

The results of the Quality Dice immediately determine the operational status of the Custom Gear. Check the Quality Dice in the following order:

*   **1 (The Rebound):** If **any** Quality Die shows a 1, the Custom Gear breaks instantly. The Oddity backfires violently, often harming the user or allies in the same Zone. The attack or defense automatically fails, regardless of other dice. **Roll a d6: on a 1-3, one attached Oddity is permanently destroyed. On a 4-6, it can be salvaged.**
*   **6 (Overclocked):** If there is no Rebound, but **any** Quality Die shows a 6, the Custom Gear wildly over-performs. The Oddity's effect is magnified (e.g., hitting all targets in a Zone instead of one), and the attack completely ignores the target's Passive Defence Dice.
*   **2-5 (The Exploit):** If all Quality Dice show 2-5, the Custom Gear functions exactly as intended. The Base Item's stats apply, and the Oddity's Tags take effect based on the result of the main dice pool.

> **Example:** A Goblin swings a Custom Heavy Weapon constructed with two Oddities at an armored knight. They roll their action pool plus two red Quality Dice. The Quality Dice show a 6 and a 4! Since there are no 1s, it doesn't break, and the 6 means the weapon overclocks, performing a magnified effect!

## Oddity Tags
Below is a sample of Oddities and their corresponding Tags.

| Oddity Name | Tag | Effect (The Exploit) | Backfire (The Rebound) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bouncy Gland** | Bouncy | The weapon can be thrown into an adjacent Zone and immediately returns to the wielder's hand. | The weapon bounces wildly out of control, hitting a random ally in the Zone for 1 Damage before breaking. |
| **Sticky Sap** | Sticky | Any target hit cannot willingly move to a new Zone during their next round. | The wielder becomes stuck to the floor, reducing their Movement to 0 next round. |
| **Spark Battery** | Shocking | Forces the target to immediately drop whatever they are holding in their hands. | The battery ruptures, shocking the wielder resulting in the **Fatigued** condition. |
| **Volatile Ooze** | Acidic | A successful hit permanently destroys the target's armor (Nullifies their Passive Defence Dice). | The ooze splatters backward, destroying the wielder's armor instead. |
| **Twitchy Muscle** | Alive | Grants +1d to hit targets in melee combat. | The weapon violently twists, dealing 1 Damage directly to the wielder's Grit. |
| **Alchemical Primer** | Explosive | Converts the standard single-target attack into an Area-of-Effect, hitting all enemies in the current Zone. | The primer detonates prematurely, dealing the weapon's base damage to the wielder and all allies in the Zone. |
| **Chilling Frost-Stone** | Freezing | The target hit gains the **Fatigued** condition and loses 1d from all subsequent Slink tests. | The wielder is subjected to the intense cold, gaining the **Fatigued** condition. |
