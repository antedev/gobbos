## 1. The Mechanical Challenge
The Gang system (the persistent roguelite metagame layer) had conflicting economies. Crafting required specific Scrap, while the Lair required abstract Loot. Furthermore, it was unclear if the game was a competitive "Cutthroat Capitalism" game or a cooperative "Commune" game regarding base building.

## 2. Phase 2 Decisions (Meso — Locked)

### 2.1 — The Gang is the Class ✅
- **Philosophy:** Gobbos is essentially a cooperative TTRPG. The Gang acts as the player's persistent "Class" that spans across generations of dying Bosses.
- **Infamy:** Equals the Class Level.
- **Shenanigans:** Merged with "Specialty." It acts as the Gang's core identity (Class Feature + Class Flaw). It is the seed of the Gang's culture, providing +1d to a specific approach, but forcing a mandatory compulsion.
- **Multiclassing:** A Gang's identity isn't permanently locked. As Bosses invest in different Stats and acquire different Quirks, the Gang organically "multiclassing" over generations.

### 2.2 — Scrap vs. Oddities ✅
- **Scrap is Abstract:** We do not track physical base materials (lumber, stones, metal). Scrap is abstract infrastructure gathered by expanding Lair influence.
- **Oddities are Physical:** These are the rare, specific, heavy pieces of loot found in a dungeon (e.g., Dragon Glands). 
- **The Tactical Choice:** During a raid, players must decide between hauling back abstract wealth (to build the Lair) or physical Oddities (to craft weird weapons).

### 2.3 — The Lair Boardgame (Co-Op Commune with Dominance) ✅
- **Philosophy:** The economy is primarily cooperative. Players pool basic wealth to upgrade the shared Lair.
- **Turf Removed:** The concept of individual Gang "Turf" has been deleted. Base building is strictly pushed to the communal Lair.
- **The Boardgame Mechanic:** The Lair acts as a mini-boardgame during downtime. While players cooperate to build facilities, they can assert their Gang's resources or Infamy to **dominate** specific parts of the Lair.
- **The Reward:** The Gang that contributed the most (or dominated) a specific Lair upgrade gets an extra, exclusive bonus from it, creating "friendly competition" within the co-op structure.
