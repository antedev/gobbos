## 6. Creative Genius Additions (The Spice!)
*Role: Creative Genius*

The foundation here is excellent—it gives the game a fantastic pacing loop (Raid -> Tally -> Upgrade -> Pitch the next Raid). Let's inject some goblin chaos into these systems to make sure it never feels like a spreadsheet!

### A. The "Pitch" Mechanic (Mouth vs. Wallet)
You mentioned selling the adventure to the Mobs like a Viking raid. This is gold.
*   **The Idea:** Before a raid, the Boss has to literally stand on a box and pitch the raid to the Grunts. 
*   **The Mechanic:** You can either pay them up front from the Hoard (safe, but expensive), or you can make a **Mouth Test** to promise them absurd riches (cheap, but dangerous). 
*   **The Catch:** If you promised them the moon with a Mouth Test and the raid fails, the Mob Mutiny gauge maxes out instantly, or they steal from your personal Hoard while you sleep. It creates a hilarious risk/reward where charismatic Bosses can run zero-budget raids but face lethal consequences if they fail.

### B. The Loot "Skim" (The Tax)
You noted that some loot should go to the Lair and some to the Gang. Goblins are greedy, so the transfer of wealth shouldn't be peaceful.
*   **The Idea:** The "Lair Tax" isn't an Excel formula; it's a physical gauntlet. 
*   **The Mechanic:** When the Gangs return, the Lair Boss (an NPC or an Elder) demands their cut. The players can choose to **"Skim"**—hiding the best loot from the tax collectors. If they succeed on a Slink test, they keep 100% of the value for their own Hoard. If they fail, the Lair guards beat them up, take the item anyway, and the Gang loses Infamy for getting caught.

### C. "Dead Gobbo" XP & The Glory of Dying
You asked if requiring a PC to survive to get Raid XP is too punishing. Yes, it might encourage players to play *too* safely, which is un-goblin-like!
*   **The Idea:** Reward dying gloriously. 
*   **The Mechanic:** If a Boss dies during the raid, their *Gang* still gets the XP from the Raid Tally, and they actually get a **+1 XP Bonus** for providing an entertaining story for the survivors to tell at the bar. This guarantees the player's new Boss starts off stronger and softens the blow of death, encouraging reckless, fun play.

### D. Goblin Bar Fights & Rumor Mill (Town Activities)
You mentioned wanting side activities so it isn't just bookkeeping.
*   **The Idea:** The "Downtime Action" system (like from *Wicked Ones* or *Blades*). Each Boss gets 1 or 2 Actions in the Lair Phase.
*   **The Activities:**
    *   **Start a Bar Fight:** Test Tough. Winner gains +1 Infamy and steals D6 Loot from the loser. Loser takes 1 Damage before the next raid even starts.
    *   **Listen for Rumors:** Pay 2 Loot to the bartender to learn the layout of the next dungeon or the weakness of a monster.
    *   **Tape Junk Together (Crafting):** Try to make a bomb out of garbage. Roll Brains. On a 1, it blows up in your face.

### E. Mob "Veterans" (Mob Levels)
You asked how different levels of Mobs would play out.
*   **The Idea:** Don't track individual Grunt XP (too much math). Instead, if a Mob survives a raid, they gain a "Veteran" trait. 
*   **The Mechanic:** Give them a random, highly specific quirk/boon. *Example: "Survived the Fire Trap." Boon: Ignore the first point of fire damage.* However, they now demand double the Loot/pay to go on the next raid because they know what they are doing. If you get a hyper-elite Boss with an expensive Veteran Mob, losing them is a monumental, hilarious tragedy.
