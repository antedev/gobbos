# Inspiration Notes (Updated for OSR focus)

As requested, I have pivoted from modern narrative games like 5e and looked into Old-School Renaissance (OSR) and B/X D&D-style reference materials. These systems focus on fast, deadly combat, rulings over rules, and resource management. Here is what is relevant for Gobbos:

## 1. Basic Fantasy RPG / Old-School Essentials (OSE)
**Why it's interesting:**
- **OSR Simplicity:** These systems strip back the rules to create fast, dangerous combat. Their mechanics are an excellent template for Gobbos.
- **Retainer/Grunt Mechanics:** OSR features standard rules for hiring retainers ("Grunts"), tracking their morale, and detailing how they follow orders. Morale checks specifically (usually rolling 2d6 under a morale score) dictate if units break or flee when hit, which is perfect for "Orders" in Gobbos.
- **Lethality:** Goblins are famously fragile, and OSR games excel at presenting extremely dangerous environments where low-level characters die easily if they don't use tactics or gang up on enemies.

## 2. OSR Mob Combat Design Advice
**Why it's interesting:**
- A compiled summary of OSR community best practices for mass combat (such as the old BECMI "War Machine" or fan-made One-Roll systems). 
- Highlights include treating a mob as a single hit die entity, simplifying attack rolls (e.g., auto-hits based on numbers), and heavily relying on Reaction and Morale rolls instead of just hit-point attrition.
- These principles should directly inspire the "Mob Flavors" and how Mobs take damage and flee in Gobbos!

## Alternate Systems (Honorable Mentions)
- **Wicked Ones (Free Edition):** A Forged in the Dark game where you *play as the monsters* building a dungeon. Great inspiration for monster-centric play.
- **Goblin Quest:** A slapstick RPG specifically about Goblins trying to accomplish fatal tasks. Good for looking at "Order Fumbles" and hilarious consequence tables.
- **Mausritter:** Contains excellent "Warband" rules where squads of mice fight as single units using very streamlined OSR rules.
