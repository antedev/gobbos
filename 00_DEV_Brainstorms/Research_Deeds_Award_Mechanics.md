# Role: The Researcher (Workflow Output)

**Topic:** TTRPG Mechanics for Acquiring Abilities via "Deeds", Specific Actions, or Achievements.
**Objective:** Identify, compare, and analyze TTRPGs that utilize achievement-based progression or specific action-triggers to award abilities, and provide objective commentary on their application to the chaotic philosophy of *Gobbos*.

## Findings: Deed-Based Progression in TTRPGs

The concept of tying specific mechanical rewards to specific in-game actions is an established, albeit niche, mechanic in TTRPG design. It is often employed to enforce genre tropes, encourage proactive player behavior, and replace traditional, math-heavy Experience Point (XP) systems.

### 1. The "Beats" System (*Heart: The City Beneath* & *Spire*)
- **Mechanic:** Characters advance not by killing monsters, but by fulfilling "Beats". These are specific narrative or mechanical goals chosen by the player (e.g., "Take major fallout," "Kill a significant enemy," "Perform a blood sacrifice"). 
- **Award:** Achieving a Minor, Major, or Zenith Beat immediately grants an advance of the corresponding tier (a specific new ability or feature).
- **Commentary:** This is highly relevant to *Gobbos*. By putting the "Deed" on the player's character sheet as a prerequisite for advancement, it incentivizes the players to drive the plot and seek out those exact chaotic situations. It is a proactive, rather than reactive, progression system.

### 2. The "Scar" Table (*Into the Odd* & *Electric Bastionland*)
- **Mechanic:** When a character is reduced to exactly 0 HP, they roll on a "Scars" table. The result is tied to the specific type of trauma they endured.
- **Award:** They might gain a weird new ability, a permanent physical alteration (which could have mechanical benefits), or a permanent change to their stats.
- **Commentary:** This aligns perfectly with the proposed "Die by Fire" or "Die because of a Fumble" deeds. It turns severe failure or near-death experiences into avenues for gaining unique Tricks, reinforcing the roguelite, lethal nature of *Gobbos*.

### 3. Condition & Obsession Mechanics (*Unknown Armies* & *Changeling: The Lost*)
- **Mechanic:** In *Unknown Armies*, spellcasters (Adepts) gain magical "charges" (the fuel for their abilities) by performing highly specific, often self-destructive or bizarre deeds (e.g., reckless driving, smoking, physical self-harm). 
- **Award:** The explicit fuel needed to use their most powerful Tricks.
- **Commentary:** While this is for *fueling* rather than *acquiring* abilities, the design philosophy is identical. It mechanically demands chaotic roleplaying.

### 4. Achievement & Destiny Milestones (*Level Up: Advanced 5E* & *Heroic Deeds RPG*)
- **Mechanic:** Characters have stated "Destinies" or specific "Deeds" tied to their archetype. Completing a set number of these specific achievements triggers a level-up.
- **Award:** Broader character advancement or specific archetype features.
- **Commentary:** This is a more traditional milestone approach. It is less modular than acquiring a single Trick for a single Deed, making it perhaps too rigid for a fast-paced Goblin game, but it validates the core concept of achievement-driven play.

## Alternatives & Pedantic Recommendations for *Gobbos*

If *Gobbos* implements "Deeds" to acquire "Tricks", the following implementation structures present the most robust alternatives:

1. **The Prerequisite Model (The "Beat" Approach):**
   List the Tricks, and beside each, list the "Deed" required to unlock it. 
   *Example:* **Trick:** *Pyromaniac's Joy* | **Deed:** Survive being set on fire, or set an enemy on fire while you are also on fire.
   *Why it's better:* Players can read the rulebook, find a Trick they want, and spend the session trying to orchestrate the chaotic event required to get it.

2. **The Death/Lineage Model (The "Roguelite" Approach):**
   Since death is a core tenet of *Gobbos* leveling, tie the Deeds to the *manner* of the previous character's death or the pinnacle achievement of their short life.
   *Example:* If a Gobbo dies by ordering a Mob beyond their Grunt capacity (leading to a Mob mutiny), the *next* Gobbo the player rolls up instantly starts with the "Authoritarian" Trick. 
   *Why it's better:* It perfectly honors Design Tenet #5 (Character death is a part of leveling up) and softens the blow of losing a character by rewarding the player for *how spectacularly* they failed.

  - *Free Rules:* [A5E SRD / Tools site](https://a5e.tools/)
