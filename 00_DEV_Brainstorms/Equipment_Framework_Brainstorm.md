# Equipment Framework Brainstorm

*Refined framework focusing on high-level concepts, generic structures, and slim mechanical tracking.*

## Core Item Attributes
Every item in the game is defined by three primary attributes. These dictate how an item interacts with the PC and the world. 

### 1. Bulk
How heavy, large, or unwieldy an item is. Bulk is the primary limiting factor for what a Gobbo can bring to or from a raid.
*   **Capacity Limit:** The total Bulk a PC can carry is determined by their **Carry Capacity** (derived from the *Tough* stat). 
*   **Scale:** Bulk generally ranges from 0 (tiny items, lockpicks, coins) to 3+ (massive statues, heavy machinery). Most standard equipment is Bulk 1 or 2.

### 2. Value
Value represents the item's worth (such as "Teeth", "Shinies", or trade value). 
*   **Acquisition & Economy:** Value has its own merit. Between adventures or raids, Value is used as a currency to acquire new equipment for the PC or to outfit their Mob. 
*   **Progression:** Surplus Value brought back to the Lair can be invested in Lair Upgrades or used to increase the Gang's Renown. 

### 3. Usage
Usage defines *how* and *when* an item interacts with the action economy. Standardizing this prevents confusion at the table.
*   **Passive:** The item provides its benefit constantly just by being worn or carried (e.g., Armor granting Passive Defence).
*   **Action:** Using the item costs one of the PC's standard Actions during their turn.
*   **Reaction / Free:** The item can be used instantly, either outside the normal action economy or in response to a specific trigger.

## The Concept of "Flair" vs "Traits"
To keep the game moving fast, standard equipment shouldn't bog down play with complex calculations. 
*   **Flair:** Purely narrative fluff. Flair describes how cool, dirty, or shiny an item looks. It has no mechanical weight but helps players visualize their Gobbo and roleplay.
*   **Mechanical Traits:** Only specific, notable items have Mechanical Traits (like "Cleave" or "Reach"). Standard equipment relies on simple dice modifiers instead.

## Equipment Categories (Conceptual)

### 1. Weapons
Weapons are tools for violence. To keep mechanics slim, a weapon's primary function is to modify the dice pool rather than introduce complex sub-systems.
*   **Standard Mechanics:** A typical weapon simply adds +1d or +2d to an attack roll.
*   **Special Rules:** We should avoid tacking on special rules to *every* weapon. Special rules (Traits) should be reserved for unique weapons or specific weapon *classes* (e.g., blackpowder weapons), so players don't have to look up unique rules for a standard sword.

### 2. Armor and Defence
Armor keeps Gobbos alive by providing pure defensive capabilities without requiring an action to use.
*   **Passive Defence:** Armor provides "Passive Defence" dice. These dice are rolled automatically against incoming attacks. 
*   *Note on Dodge:* This is distinct from "Active Dodge." **Active Dodge** is an intentional action fueled by the **Slink** stat, whereas **Passive Defence** just relies on wearing the armor.
*   **Ablative Concept:** Armor might have a mechanic where it can be sacrificed or broken to negate massive damage, serving as a consumable life-saver. 
(Comment: this might be a bit complex for Mobs, but perhaps a good thing for PC)

### 3. Consumables
Single-use items that are highly volatile and chaotic (Brews, Shrooms, Explosives).
*   **Mechanics:** High impact, but often carry a risk or require a chaotic roll to see if they backfire. Used via an Action or Free/Reaction depending on the item. 

### 4. Utility / Tools
Items designed to interact with the environment or bypass obstacles, rather than for direct combat.
*   **Mechanics:** Often provide flat benefits to specific tests (e.g., lockpicks turning a Normal test into an Easy test) or allow actions that would otherwise be impossible.

### 5. Pure Treasure
Items that exist solely to be carried out of the dungeon.
*   **Mechanics:** High Value, High Bulk, zero utility. Creates the classic dilemma of managing Carry Capacity versus greed.
