# Researcher Findings: Base & Lair Building Mechanics

**Role:** The Researcher
**Objective:** Analyze the best mechanical implementations of base/domain building in existing TTRPGs to inspire the "Lair Phase" in Gobbos.

## Top Contenders & Mechanics

### 1. Blades in the Dark (Forged in the Dark)
*Blades in the Dark* treats the criminal Crew and its Lair as a shared player-character.
*   **The "Claims" Map (Turf Expansion):** Instead of just buying upgrades from a list, the Lair has a visual web/map of "Claims" (e.g., *Fighting Pits, Smuggling Route, Informant Network*). You start in the center. To get an upgrade, you have to do a raid to conquer an adjacent node on the map. 
    *   *Gobbo Application:* Instead of just paying "Loot" for a Blacksmith, the Gangs look at a Lair Map and say "We need to clear out the *Flooded Caves* node to build a Mushroom Farm." It turns base building into a physical turf war.
*   **Crew Upgrades:** Modular, checklist-style upgrades to the physical Lair (Vaults to hold more wealth, Quarters to heal faster).

### 2. Mutant: Year Zero (Free League)
This game features "The Ark," a central hub where the post-apocalyptic survivors live.
*   **Collaborative "Projects":** During the Ark Phase, all players vote on a collective Project (e.g., building a Palisade, a Water Purifier, or a Defensible Gate). Players then assign their characters to work on it during Downtime to advance a "Progress Clock."
*   **Development Levels (DEV):** The Ark has four stats: *Food Supply, Culture, Technology,* and *Warfare*. Completing Projects raises these stats. You cannot build advanced Projects (like a Generator) until the Ark's Technology stat is high enough.
    *   *Gobbo Application:* The Lair itself has "Dev Levels." If the Gangs want advanced weapons from the shop, they must collectively raise the Lair's "Scrap-Tech" level first.

### 3. Wicked Ones
A game where players are specifically playing monsters running a dungeon. Highly relevant.
*   **Lurking vs. Roaming Phases:** Hard mechanical distinction between being in the dungeon (Lurking) and going on raids (Roaming). 
*   **Dungeon Discoveries:** As players spend downtime strictly "digging" to expand their lair to build new rooms, the GM rolls for Discoveries. They might unearth an underground river, a hostile insect hive, or raw minerals. 
    *   *Gobbo Application:* Expanding the Lair isn't just spending money; it's manual labor that can accidentally trigger mini-encounters right inside the home base. 
*   **Downtime Action Economy:** Players get exactly 2 Downtime actions to spend on Lair upkeep, trap building, or brewing potions. 

### 4. Forbidden Lands (Free League)
Focuses on OSR-style hexcrawling and taking over an empty castle to build a "Stronghold."
*   **Functions & Hirelings:** You can build physical "Functions" in your Stronghold (e.g., a Forge, a Mill, a Guard Tower). However, the Function does nothing unless you assign a specific NPC Hireling to run it (a Smith, a Miller, a Guard Captain).
    *   *Gobbo Application:* This perfectly validates our brainstormed "Elder" mechanic. A Lair Upgrade is useless unless a player officially retires their Boss as an Elder and assigns them to run the facility. 

## Synthesis & Recommendations for Gobbos

If we want the Lair to be *easy to use* but mechanically rich, we should steal the following framework:

1.  **The Phase Split (Wicked Ones):** Hard sequence of play. `The Raid Phase` (Action) -> `The Lair Phase` (Base Building/Upkeep/Healing).
2.  **The Claims Web (Blades in the Dark):** The Lair Upgrades shouldn't just be a spreadsheet. It should be a visual "Tech Tree" or Map. To unlock the *Goblin Catapult*, you must first unlock the *Scrap Yard* node. 
3.  **The Hireling Tax (Forbidden Lands):** To permanently activate a major node on the Claims Web, a Boss must be retired into an Elder to run it. This creates a fantastic economy where players must choose between keeping a Level 6 Boss for personal glory, or retiring them to give the whole table a massive Lair upgrade.
