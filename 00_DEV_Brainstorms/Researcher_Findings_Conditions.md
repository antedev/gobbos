# Researcher Findings: Common TTRPG Conditions & Status Effects

**Role:** The Researcher
**Objective:** Analyze the most common and essential status conditions used in major TTRPGs (like D&D 5e and Pathfinder 2e) to provide a curated list for the Gobbos system.

## The Consensus: The "Top 8" Essential Conditions
Across the most popular tactical TTRPGs, the following conditions form the mechanical backbone of combat debuffs. I have grouped them thematically and suggested how they might cleanly map to Gobbos' existing Mechanics (Tough, Slink, Brains, Mouth, Notice, and Movement).

### 1. Blinded / Dazzled (Sensory)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Disadvantage/Penalties on attacks; enemies get advantage against the blinded target. Auto-fail on sight-based checks.
*   **Gobbo Application:** You already have this listed (`-1 on any Physical tests and Notice`). Very standard and universally applied.

### 2. Restrained / Grappled (Mobility)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Speed drops to 0. Penalties to Dexterity (Slink) and attacks.
*   **Gobbo Application:** You have *Restrained* as `-1d on Slink tests`. You should also explicitly state that their **Movement becomes 0** (or they cannot Move as a Standard Action). 

### 3. Frightened / Terrified (Morale)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Cannot willingly move closer to the source of fear. Penalties to all checks/attacks while in line of sight of the source.
*   **Gobbo Application:** A goblin staple. *Terrified:* "Cannot move closer to the source of fear. -1d to all attacks/tests against the source."

### 4. Prone (Positioning)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Being knocked down. Costs movement to stand up. Melee attacks against the prone target have an advantage; ranged attacks have a disadvantage (because they are a smaller target). 
*   **Gobbo Application:** *Prone:* "Costs a Standard Action (or 1 Movement) to stand up. Enemies get +1d to melee attacks against you."

### 5. Poisoned / Sickened (Biological)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Flat penalty/disadvantage to all attacks and ability checks. Sometimes causes persistent damage at the end of a turn.
*   **Gobbo Application:** *Poisoned:* "-1d on all Tough and Slink tests" OR "Take 1 Damage at the end of your turn until you pass a Tough test."

### 6. Stunned / Incapacitated (Action Economy)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** The harshest condition. The creature cannot take actions or reactions, cannot move, and automatically fails physical saves. 
*   **Gobbo Application:** *Stunned:* "You lose your next Standard Action. Enemies get +1d to attack you." (Keep it simple since Gobbos action economy is precious).

### 7. Exhausted / Enfeebled (Fatigue)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Often a tiered system (D&D) or flat penalty to Strength (Pathfinder). 
*   **Gobbo Application:** *Weakened* (which you have): `-1d on Tough tests`. This perfectly covers the exhaustion/enfeebled archetype.

### 8. Flat-Footed / Off-Guard (Defense/Distracted)
*   **How it works elsewhere:** Represents being flanked or surprised. Lowers Armor Class / Defense.
*   **Gobbo Application:** *Confused* (which you have): `-1d on Brains tests`. We could expand "Distracted/Off-Guard" to mean enemies roll +1d to attack you because your guard is down.
