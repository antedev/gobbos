# Researcher Findings: Optimal OSR Source Material 

**Objective:** The user requested further research into acquiring specific OSR (Old-School Renaissance) rulebooks and documents, specifically providing the example of Moldvay B/X D&D hosted on AnyFlip (`https://anyflip.com/inssi/oitr/basic`). The goal is to provide reliable, accessible sources to inspire the "Gobbos" system's mechanics (Mob combat, Orders, Morale).

## 1. Analysis of AnyFlip and Document Sharing Sites
*   **The AnyFlip Example:** The provided URL `https://anyflip.com/inssi/oitr/basic` successfully links to an uploaded scan of the **1981 Dungeons & Dragons Basic Box Set** (often called the Moldvay Basic rules). 
*   **The Problem with Document Hubs:** While AnyFlip, FlipHTML5, and VK occasionally host these documents, they suffer from three major drawbacks for a game designer:
    1.  **Obfuscation:** Uploaders intentionally disguise names (e.g., "DND bx" instead of the actual title) to avoid copyright takedowns, making targeted searching extremely difficult.
    2.  **Usability:** Scanned PDFs hosted in flip-book viewers cannot be easily searched (Ctrl+F), excerpted, or copy-pasted into your own design notes.
    3.  **Legality & Reliability:** Links frequently suffer from DMCA takedowns and rot, meaning you might lose access to your reference material mid-design.

## 2. The Researcher's Recommendation: Official Free SRDs
As a Researcher, my job is to find the *best practice* and most reliable sources. Fortunately, the OSR movement is predominantly open-source. Instead of relying on obfuscated AnyFlip links, **there are vastly superior, 100% legal, profoundly accessible, and free alternatives** that contain the exact mechanics requested.

### Strategy 1: The "B/X D&D" Foundation (For Morale & Grunts)
Rather than reading a scanned 1981 PDF on AnyFlip, you should use modern retro-clones. These games mathematically recreate the exact B/X rules but format them for modern readers.
*   **Old-School Essentials (OSE) SRD:** 
    *   **Source:** [oldschoolessentials.necroticgnome.com/srd](https://oldschoolessentials.necroticgnome.com/srd)
    *   **Why it's better:** This is the current gold standard. It is a completely free, hyperlinked, searchable wiki containing all B/X D&D rules. It perfectly outlines Morale rules (2d6 checks) and Retainer/Mercenary (Grunt) rules.
*   **Basic Fantasy RPG:**
    *   **Source:** [basicfantasy.org/downloads.html](https://www.basicfantasy.org/downloads.html)
    *   **Why it's better:** The entire game system and dozens of community expansions are available as **free high-quality PDF downloads** on their official site. It streamlines the Moldvay B/X rules for faster play.

### Strategy 2: Modular Rules-Light Systems (For Fast Combat & Brutality)
If the goal is to find games with blazing fast combat (auto-hits) and modular "inventory-as-class" mechanics for Gobbos, these systems are given away for free by their creators.
*   **Cairn RPG (First & Second Edition):**
    *   **Source:** [cairnrpg.com/cairn-srd](https://cairnrpg.com/cairn-srd/)
    *   **Why it's better:** Cairn combines the inventory system of *Knave* with the auto-hit, incredibly fast combat of *Into the Odd*. The entire text is CC-BY-SA 4.0 licensed, meaning the HTML rules are completely free on their website. 

## 3. Conclusion & Next Steps
**Stop searching AnyFlip.** While it worked as a one-off for the Mörk Borg or B/X PDF, you will spend more time fighting the search engine than designing your game. 

**Recommendation for Gobbos Design:**
1.  Open the **OSE SRD**, navigate strictly to the *Encounters* and *Retainers* sections, and copy their 2d6 Morale mechanics to finalize the **Orders** system.
2.  Open the **Cairn RPG SRD**, read the *Combat* section (which features auto-damage), and use this as the baseline for your **Mob Combat** interactions to keep goblin fights terrifyingly fast.
