# Researcher Findings: Optimal OSR Frameworks for Gobbos

**Objective:** To analyze and recommend tabletop roleplaying game (TTRPG) frameworks that align with the design goals of "Gobbos"—specifically, a requirement for high lethality, fast resolution, ruling-over-rules mechanics, and a focus on inventory and retainers over complex character sheets or narrative superpowers.

## 1. The Core Problem with Modern Fantasy (e.g., D&D 5e)
As initially hypothesized, modern narrative fantasy systems are fundamentally incompatible with the "Goblin" experience. These systems prioritize:
*   **Heroic attrition:** Characters have massive health pools and are expected to survive 6-8 encounters per day.
*   **Action bloat:** Turns take too long due to bonus actions, reactions, and complex spellcasting.
*   **Inherent power:** Characters gain power intrinsically through leveling, rather than extrinsically through loot or clever environmental interaction.
Goblins, by their nature, should be fragile, cowardly, and reliant on numbers, dirty tricks, and scrounged gear.

## 2. The Solution: Classless, Inventory-Driven OSR
To properly model the "Gobbos" experience—where Mob combat, Orders, and Loot capacity are paramount—we must look to the minimalist branches of the Old-School Renaissance (OSR).

### A. Knave (by Ben Milton)
*   **Key Mechanic:** There are no classes. Your character's role is entirely defined by their inventory. A goblin holding a wand is a wizard; a goblin holding a shield is a tank. 
*   **Loot Capacity:** Knave has an exceptionally elegant, slot-based inventory system tied to the Constitution stat. This directly solves the "Loot capacity" design goal for Gobbos. Pushing limits (carrying heavy loot) actively penalizes the character's ability to survive.
*   **Verdict:** *Highly Recommended.* The slot-based inventory is the perfect engine for greedy goblins hauling junk.

### B. Into the Odd / Cairn (by Chris McDowall / Yochai Gal)
*   **Key Mechanic:** Combat completely bypasses the "to-hit" roll. If an attack is made, it automatically deals damage, subtracted by armor.
*   **Lethality:** Because damage is guaranteed, combat is extremely fast and terrifyingly lethal. Players learn to avoid fair fights at all costs—the quintessential goblin strategy.
*   **Verdict:** *Recommended for Combat Engine.* The auto-damage mechanic perfectly models chaotic, brutal Mob combat, cutting down dice rolling by half.

### C. Mörk Borg (by Ockull and Nohr) 
*(Reference PDF successfully acquired via Anyflip)*
*   **Key Mechanic:** A brutally rules-light system where the world is actively ending. It utilizes a "player-facing" roll system; the Game Master never rolls dice for monster attacks or defenses (players roll to attack, and roll to defend).
*   **Morale:** Highly developed morale tables for enemies (and retainers), dictating when they break and run.
*   **Verdict:** *Recommended for Tone and Player-Facing Mechanics.* Having the GM never roll dice speeds up Mob combat significantly, allowing the GM to focus entirely on narration.

## 3. Secondary OSR Concepts (Basic Fantasy, Dolmenwood, Land of Eem)
While direct Anyflip PDFs for these specific games are tightly controlled or unavailable, their design philosophies offer further inspiration:

### A. Basic Fantasy RPG / Old School Essentials (OSE) / Old D&D
*   **Key Mechanic:** Strict B/X D&D recreation. Race-as-class (in OSE/Old D&D) or simplified race/class splits (Basic Fantasy). 
*   **The Grind:** These systems emphasize the "dungeon turn" (tracking torches, wandering monsters, and rations strictly).
*   **Verdict:** Excellent for structural pacing. We should ensure Gobbos has a strict tracker for resource depletion to keep the pressure high.

### B. Dolmenwood
*   **Key Mechanic:** A deeply integrated hex-crawl built on top of OSE rules, focusing on localized flavor, fairy tales, and specific regional Gangs over generic fantasy.
*   **Verdict:** A great reminder that the "flavor" of the world matters. The "Mob Flavors" in Gobbos (Banes and Boons) should feel intensely grounded in goblin lore rather than generic fantasy tropes.

### C. Land of Eem / Oathhammer
*   **Key Mechanic (Land of Eem):** Lighthearted, whimsical TTRPG designed around simple resolutions and narrative fun.
*   **Verdict:** Goblin games need comedy! "Order Fumbles" and hilarious failures should be a core mechanic to break up the lethality.

## 4. Recommended Amalgamation for Gobbos
Based on objective analysis, Gobbos should adopt:
1.  **Inventory-as-Class (from Knave):** Ditch standard character sheets in favor of a slot-based inventory grid.
2.  **Auto-Damage or Player-Facing Rolls (from Into the Odd / Mörk Borg):** Streamline Mob combat by removing the GM's need to roll d20s for every attacking enemy.
3.  **Strict Morale (from B/X D&D):** Retainers (Grunts) and enemies must constantly test morale. Goblins are not fearless; they run away. The "Orders" mechanic should directly interact with a Grunt's Morale score.
