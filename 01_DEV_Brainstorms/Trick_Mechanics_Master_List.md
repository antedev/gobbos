# Trick Mechanics Master List

This is a brainstormed list of purely mechanical effects for Tricks, stripped of flavor text and balanced considerations, organized by category.

## 1. Boons & Dice Manipulation
*   **Static Bonus:** +1d on [Specific Action/Skill/Weapon type] (e.g., +1d on Dodge, +1d on Shooting).
*   **Conditional Bonus:** +1d on tests when you are at exactly 1 Grit.
*   **Penalty to Enemies:** Enemies get -1d when attacking you with [Ranged/Melee].
*   **Rerolls:** Reroll 1 failed die on a [Specific Stat] test.
*   **Target Adjustment:** Treat one 4 as a 5 / Treat one failure as a success on [Action].
*   **Exploding Dice:** If you roll a 6 on [Action], roll an additional die.
*   **Crit manipulation:** On a roll with multiple 5s/6s, inflict a free [Status] (e.g., Bleeding, Stunned, Prone) in addition to normal damage.
*   **Success Multiplier:** When attacking with [Weapon], 6s count as two successes instead of one.
*   **Lowest Die Drop:** Discard the lowest die result from your pool instead of accepting a failure.
*   **Pushing:** Take 1 Grit damage to reroll all failed dice on a crucial test.
*   **Critical Shift:** 5s and 6s count as critical successes instead of just 6s.
*   **Fail Forward:** When you fail a roll completely (no successes), gain 1 Grunt, 1 Grit, or +1d on your next test.
*   **Shared Success:** If you generate more successes than required for a test, grant an ally +1d on their next action.

## 2. Economy Bending (Actions & Time)
*   **Conditional Extra Action:** Do an extra action when you only have one Grit left.
*   **Momentum Action:** Move 1 zone as a Free Action after performing a successful Melee Attack.
*   **Cleave Action:** If you reduce an enemy to 0 HP, immediately make another Attack action (Limit: once per turn).
*   **Free Object Interaction:** Swap weapons, ready a thrown weapon, or drink a potion as a Free Action.
*   **Reaction Attack:** When an enemy misses you with a Melee Attack, make one Melee Attack against them as a Reaction.
*   **Free Recovery:** Stand up from Prone as a Free Action.
*   **Action Downgrade:** Perform [Specific Standard Action] (e.g., Hide/Sneak) as a Free Action once per turn.
*   **Interrupt:** Activate this Trick to take your turn immediately before an enemy acts in the initiative order.
*   **Quick Reload:** Reloading a [Specific Weapon Type] is a Free Action instead of a standard action.
*   **Double Move:** Move 2 zones using a single Move action, but suffer -1d to any attacks made later this turn.
*   **Preparation:** If you do not make any attack actions this turn, you gain an extra Standard Action on your *next* turn.

## 3. Rule Breaking & Bypassing
*   **Auto-Pass:** Can climb without making a Slink test.
*   **Stat Swapping:** Use [Stat X] instead of [Stat Y] for [Specific Action] (e.g., Roll Brains instead of Brawn to attack with an odd weapon).
*   **Property Ignorance:** Ignore the "Heavy" penalty on weapons.
*   **Property Ignorance 2:** Ignore the "Loud" property on specific weapons.
*   **Constraint Breaking:** You can wield a Two-Handed weapon in one hand.
*   **Movement Bypassing:** Move through enemy zones without triggering attacks of opportunity or movement penalties.
*   **Hazard Bypassing:** You do not trigger traps that rely on weight or tripwires.
*   **Damage Mitigation:** Ignore the first point of fall damage / ignore the first X meters of falling.
*   **Size Matters:** Treat yourself as one Size category larger for the purposes of grappling, carrying, or being shoved.
*   **Cover Ignoral:** Your ranged attacks ignore aiming penalties and automatically bypass Partial Cover.
*   **Blind Fighting:** Ignore all penalties for fighting in darkness or against invisible enemies.
*   **Wall Runner:** You can end your move action on vertical surfaces without falling down immediately.
*   **Unarmed Lethality:** Your unarmed strikes deal actual damage (e.g., 2 points instead of 1 or 0) and can inflict damage statuses like a real weapon.

## 4. Resource Manipulation (Grit, Grunt, & Costs)
*   **Capacity Increase:** Gain +1 Max Grunt capacity.
*   **Cost Reduction:** Reduce the cost of [Specific Order] by 1 Grunt (minimum 1).
*   **Grunt for Dice:** Spend 1 Grunt to gain +1d on any test (can be declared after rolling).
*   **Grunt for Defense:** Spend 1 Grunt to reduce incoming damage by 1.
*   **Refunds:** Regain 1 Grunt when you critically succeed on an attack.
*   **Desperation Buff:** When you spend your last Grunt, gain +1d on all actions until the end of the turn.
*   **Auto-Success:** Spend 1 Grunt to automatically pass a Morale test for your Mob.
*   **Overcharge:** Spend 2 Grunt to add an extra d6 to a pool that normally cannot exceed a specific hard limit.
*   **Efficient Leader:** Once per raid, issue an Order without spending any Grunt points.
*   **Blood for Grunt:** Take 1 Grit point of damage to instantly gain 1 Grunt.
*   **Rest Recovery:** When completing a short rest/breather, regain +1 extra Grunt or Grit on top of the normal roll.

## 5. Mob & Leadership
*   **Meat Shield:** When you take damage, you can assign it to a Grunt in your current Mob instead (costs 1 Action or active Reaction).
*   **Mob Speed:** Grunts in your Mob gain +1 to their Speed or Move distance.
*   **Mob Boons:** Mobs you lead roll +1d when making a specific attack (e.g., Volley/Ranged).
*   **Penalty Negation:** Mobs you lead do not suffer penalties when at half-size or under.
*   **Free Merging:** You can merge two smaller friendly Mobs into one as a Free Action.
*   **Casualty Resistance:** Mobs you lead ignore the first casualty they take each combat/raid.
*   **Rallying Cry:** Spend an Action. Any broken, fleeing, or panicking Mobs in your zone automatically rally and reform.
*   **Loot Carriers:** Grunts in your Mob can carry 1 extra Loot item each.
*   **Forced March:** A Mob can accompany you on a Dash/Double Move without scattering or falling behind.
*   **Living Wall:** Mobs you lead automatically provide you with Full Cover from Ranged attacks as long as they have 3+ Grunts remaining.
*   **Suicide Charge:** Order a Mob to attack with +2d, but they automatically take 1 casualty after the attack resolves due to their reckless frenzy.

## 6. Survivability & Defense
*   **Armor Bypass:** Ignore the "Clunky" penalty of Heavy Armor.
*   **Damage Type Resistance:** Ignore the first point of damage from [Specific Damage Type, e.g., Fire/Explosions] each turn.
*   **Death Defy:** When taking damage that would reduce Grit to 0, roll 1d6. On a 5+, stay at 1 Grit. (Limit: once per raid).
*   **Vampiric:** Gain 1 temporary Grit when defeating an enemy.
*   **Hard to Kill:** When reduced to 0 Grit, you can still take Actions on your next turn before officially dying (Last Stand).
*   **Fast Healing:** Regain 1 Grit automatically at the start of any combat encounter.
*   **Status Immunity:** You can never be [Stunned / Bleeding / Poisoned / Prone] (pick one).
*   **Iron Stomach:** You can eat spoiled rations, dungeon mold, or weird junk to regain Grit without suffering sickness penalties.
*   **Evasive Roll:** If you take damage from an explosion or an Area-of-Effect attack, reduce it by half (rounded up) or avoid it entirely if you pass a quick Slink test.

## 7. Loot & Gear Interaction
*   **Scavenger:** When searching a room or body for Loot, you always find at least one minor item automatically in addition to what's rolled.
*   **Jury-Rigger:** You can repair a broken weapon, armor, or item with random scrap in the middle of a raid.
*   **Pack Mule:** You can carry +2 additional Bulky items without suffering encumbrance penalties.
*   **Weapon Juggler:** You can equip two different weapons and alternate between them without spending actions to "Swap."
*   **Explosives Expert:** Bombs, potions, and throwables you use have their effect radius increased by 1 zone.
